Our Story

We are four female biologists and scientists from Porto but we are passionate about different fields: Sara and Conceição live and breathe ecotoxicology and Olga and Rita fell in love with microbiology.

PINKtech started way back in 2015, in Universidade do Porto, when we decided to join efforts and our knowledge and started to explore the possible use of our bacteria as food for aquatic organisms. We kicked off by feeding a small planktonic crustacean, Daphnia magna, our model organism. We realised that we could be on to something interesting as soon as we started to see the Daphnias turning pink.

After several assays with this crustacean, we started feeding zebra fishes with our bacteria and started to get really excited with the potential of our little microorganisms!

In the end of 2019, PINKtech was officially born when we decided to participate in a StartUp competition for Researchers of UPTEC… And we won!! Since then, we participated in two more Start-Up competitions: BluAct Portugal (2nd place) and ClimateKIC Launched Portugal (finalist).

We are still developing our product and are looking for partners that want to try PINKtech or get involved with our project in any other way, if you want to know more about, visit our Contact page and talk to us!

Our Values & Our Dream

We want to create a sustainable, efficient, science-based solution for your aquafeed solutions. The PINKtech team is committed to the emergent need to protect the planet and the ecosystems and a circular economy concept.
Our dream is to implement PINKtech in a broad range of aqua fisheries, helping the environment and fish stocks and the human communities that depends on aqua business

Our Product as a unique solution

PINKtech is a natural food complement for aquatic organisms. It is composed by bacteria that are naturally rich in carotenoids and fatty acids. PINKtech can be used as direct feeding (shrimps or fish, e.g.) or as indirect feeding (daphnia, e.g., that after is fed to fish).
Lab tests have demonstrated that complement feeding with PINKtech increase reproduction output in 75% and decreases mortality in 30%. The carotenoids present in PINKtech are acquired by the organisms, who demonstrated the pink coloration after the feeding.
We propose a new feeding solution that can improve overall well-being of the organisms and increase their market value.
